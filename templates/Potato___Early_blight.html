Early Blight in Potatoes

Early blight is a common fungal disease affecting potato plants, leading to reduced yield and compromised plant health.

About Early Blight

Early blight, caused by the fungus Alternaria solani, presents as dark, concentric rings on leaves and tubers, resembling a target. Affected foliage may yellow and wither, severely impacting plant vitality and productivity.

Treatment and Prevention

To manage early blight, consider the following practices:

  • Rotate crops annually to prevent disease recurrence.
  • Ensure adequate plant spacing to improve air circulation.
  • Apply fungicides as necessary, focusing on those labeled for early blight control.
  • Regularly remove and destroy affected plant parts to reduce the spread.
  • Mulch around the base of plants to prevent spore splash-back during rain.
