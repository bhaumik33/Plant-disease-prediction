Late Blight in Potatoes

Late blight, caused by the pathogen Phytophthora infestans, is notorious for its role in the Irish Potato Famine. It remains a major threat to potato crops worldwide.

About Late Blight

Late blight affects the leaves, stems, and fruit of potato plants, presenting as dark, water-soaked lesions that rapidly enlarge. Under humid conditions, a white fungal growth can appear, leading to rapid decay of the plant.

Management and Control

Effective management of late blight involves a combination of strategies:

  • Using certified disease-free seed potatoes.
  • Implementing crop rotation and proper field sanitation to reduce pathogen sources.
  • Applying fungicides strategically to protect plants during susceptible growth stages.
  • Monitoring weather conditions and adjusting management practices accordingly.

Early detection and immediate action are critical in preventing widespread crop loss.
