Tomato Leaf Mold

Tomato Leaf Mold is a fungal disease that primarily affects greenhouse-grown tomatoes, caused by Passalora fulva. It manifests as pale green or yellow spots on the upper leaf surface, with a distinctive velvety, olive-green to brown mold on the underside.

About Tomato Leaf Mold

This disease thrives in humid conditions and can cause significant yield loss if not managed properly. Leaf Mold can spread rapidly, affecting the photosynthetic ability of the plant and weakening it over time.

Management and Prevention

Effective control of Tomato Leaf Mold involves:

  • Maintaining lower humidity levels within the greenhouse.
  • Ensuring proper ventilation and air circulation.
  • Practicing crop rotation and using resistant tomato varieties.
  • Applying fungicides as necessary, following integrated pest management (IPM) guidelines.
  • Regularly inspecting plants for early signs of infection and removing affected leaves promptly.
