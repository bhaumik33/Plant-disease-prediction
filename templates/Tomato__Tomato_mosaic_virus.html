Tomato Mosaic Virus

Tomato Mosaic Virus (ToMV) is a viral disease that affects tomato plants, leading to distorted growth, mosaic patterns on leaves, and reduced yields.

About Tomato Mosaic Virus

The virus can spread through contact with infected plants or tools, and it remains viable in dried plant debris and on surfaces. Symptoms include mottling and distortion of leaves, stunted growth, and abnormal fruit development.

Management and Control

Managing ToMV involves:

  • Practicing strict hygiene and sanitation to prevent spread.
  • Using virus-free seeds and resistant tomato varieties.
  • Removing and destroying infected plants immediately.
  • Cleaning tools and equipment to remove viral particles.
  • Avoiding smoking around tomato plants, as tobacco can carry the virus.

Preventative measures are crucial, as there are no chemical treatments available for viral diseases in plants.
