Early Blight in Tomatoes

Early blight, a fungal disease caused by Alternaria solani, commonly affects tomato plants, leading to reduced vigor and yield.

About Early Blight

Early blight affects tomato plants by causing dark, concentric circles on leaves, stems, and fruits. These symptoms often start on the lower leaves and can spread rapidly under moist conditions.

Management and Prevention

Controlling early blight involves a mix of cultural practices and timely interventions:

  • Avoid overhead watering to reduce leaf wetness.
  • Practice crop rotation to minimize soil-borne pathogens.
  • Use mulch to prevent spores from splashing onto leaves from the soil.
  • Apply fungicides when necessary, especially during humid or rainy seasons.
  • Remove and destroy infected plant parts to reduce the spread of the disease.
